PLATO Hand: Shaping Contact Behavior with Fingernails for Precise Manipulation

Anonymous Author1,

Abstract

We introduces the PLATO Hand, a dexterous robotic hand with a hybrid fingertip that embeds a rigid fingernail within a compliant pulp. This design shapes contact behavior to enable diverse interaction modes across a range of object geometries. We develop a strain-energy-based bending–indentation model to guide the fingertip design and to explain how guided contact preserves local indentation while suppressing global bending. Experimental results show that the proposed robotic hand demonstrates improved pinching stability, enhanced force observability, and successful execution of edge-sensitive manipulation tasks, including paper singulation, card picking, and orange peeling. Together, these results show that coupling structured contact geometry with force-motion transparent mechanism provides a principled, physically-embodied approach to precise manipulation.

Design Overview

The PLATO Hand combines a hybrid fingertip with proprioceptive actuation for high-bandwidth force-regulated interaction. The fingertip integrates a rigid fingernail within a compliant pulp, separating local contact compliance from global fingertip stiffness. This enables fluid transitions between precise force control and adaptive grasping.

Strain-Energy Model

We develop an energy-based model that partitions fingertip deformation into global bending and local indentation. The model reveals how material stiffness and contact curvature govern deformation distribution. Higher fingernail stiffness drives deformation into local indentation, enhancing contact stability and force transmission.

Experimental Results

Pinching Stability

Pull-out experiments demonstrate 23–78% improvement in pinching stability across planar and curved surfaces. The fingernail consistently increases maximum pull-out force, with effects more pronounced on curved geometries.

Force Sensing

The hybrid fingertip exhibits 8 dB increase at low frequencies and over 20 dB at high frequencies during texture sensing. Proprioceptive force estimation tracks distal measurements with errors below 5% during high-speed impacts.

Manipulation Tasks

Edge-sensitive tasks achieve over 80% success with the fingernail but fail completely without it. Evaluated tasks include paper singulation, coin picking, card picking, card flipping, lid opening, and orange peeling.
